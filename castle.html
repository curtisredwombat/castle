Exploring the Siege Camp

Outside the walls, you can see Baron Von Hess's encampment. Observe the tents and supply wagons necessary for a prolonged siege. Can you spot the trebuchet, a powerful siege engine used to bombard the castle walls?

The Attack on the Outer Bailey

Focus on the outer walls of Oakhaven Keep. Imagine the attackers attempting to scale the wooden palisade using ladders, while defenders rain down arrows from the guard towers. The shield-bearing soldiers would have provided some protection for those placing the ladders.

Breaching the Main Gate

Examine the main gate and the raised drawbridge. The battering ram, likely sheltered to protect the soldiers pushing it, would have been the primary tool to try and break through the gate. Defenders above would have used murder holes to drop dangerous materials on the attackers.

Defenses of Oakhaven Keep

Look closely at the castle walls and towers. Notice the arrow slits that allowed defenders to fire upon the attackers while remaining relatively protected. The strong central keep served as a final refuge if the outer defenses were breached.
